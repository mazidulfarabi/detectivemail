Two Detectives

Cases, Criminal Gangs / By Md Mazidul Haque Farabi

The death of a local MP has shocked almost everyone, and now people are in full suspense about what might be going on. While you might think this was an act by the political beneficiaries of his death, everyone here, including officer McGill taking a sip of his morning coffee at the Dublin Police Department, is absolutely sure that its not. The case very certainly needed higher investigation, and so he had to make a call. He and his detective team had to reach a satisfactory reason, possibly with names, of the murder within 48-72 hours or risk being suspended. So he sought advice from his senior, and they both agreed upon bringing two qualified foreign detectives to help them investigate the case.

Detective Mr. Alexander Ivan from the Russian Crimes & Forensics Branch and detective Kevin Miles from the New York Police Department arrived at Dublin on the first available flight to Dublin the next day. Though officer McGill wasnt that worried about a Russian and an American working together, his senior did alert him about some of the problems he may have to face because of the distinct difference in nationalities. Although there was nothing, he could do as these two detectives were assigned by the higher security intelligence of the Irish Government upon his seniors request. The detectives took different flights, but they were staying in two adjacent rooms of the same hotel.

At Dublin PD;

Officer McGill: Detective Miles, please take a seat.
Mr. Miles: Thank you, officer. So, I heard its pretty bad.
McGill: (Exhaling) It is. And the worst part is, we still have no clue on why, nothing at all.
Miles: Dont worry, officer, well find out something.
McGill: We better do.
Miles: Um, by the way, I heard the other guys Russian.
McGill: Yeah, I know, look, Mr. Alex is a great detective. And its just one case, dont make any trouble, will you?
Miles: Oh, Ill try my best, officer. But you know Russians.
McGill: So, we couldve declared that it was suicide but, OESZA.
Miles: What is that?
McGill: Some kind of Criminal Organization, I dont know. It was written with white paint on the street exactly where he died.
Miles: And you said there was no political involvement; why?
McGill: That is because MP Smith was a great guy. He had no competitors in Dublin in the last election. His family confirmed that he also had no such enmity with anyone that they could recall.
Miles: Understood.

Miles met the other detective, Mr. Ivan, in the hotel lobby. He would get his stuff from his room to investigate the crime scene, but he then thought theyll both go together.

Miles: Hi, you must be detective Alex, from your Russian looks.
Alexander: First time we speak, and you had to bring that up.
Miles: Yeah. You must be 5 years older than me.
Alexander: And I request you behave that way.
Miles: Hum, okay.
Alexander: Now, do you have any updates on the case?
Miles: OESZA, thats all we got.
Alexander: No relevant information on Google?
Miles: Nope.
Alexander: Lets go.

They both reached the crime scene a few moments later. Alex started looking at the place of death highlighted by the cops. The area was nicely taped, and it was actually in the middle of a street-side market.

Alex: The criminal was an expert shooter. Couldve shot anyone else, but he didnt miss his target. This is definitely a criminal organization.
Miles: Could be a hitman.
Alex: You mean assassin?
Miles: Yes.
Alex: No. Assassins ought to take less risk. He couldve shot him anywhere else, but he shot him here. In the middle of a market. Makes me think it was some sort of a distraction.
Miles: Create a distraction by killing the MP? Obviously, no way.
Alex: Unless, of course, OESZA wants to prove a point.
Miles: What kind of pint?
Alex: That they are capable of doing something like this.
Miles: Interesting. And what do you think theyll gain by proving this point?
Alex: Fear.

They examined the place for any suspicious objects but found nothing.

Miles: The forensic results just came out.
Alex: M24?
Miles: Yes. How did you know?
Alex: The way his body fell tells us the angle he was shot. Look, top right corner. This means he was shot precisely from the opposite direction or the other supplementary angle.
Miles: Oh. And which is that way.
Alex: Exactly. And then look at the tall buildings that way. Two buildings. But the bullet wasnt shot from the roof. Because first of all, its not that tall, and secondly, shooting him on the ground would mean lowering his gun quite a bit, which would mean someone definitely seeing him during the shot.
Miles: But the police confirmed that there were no eye suspects.
Alex: Yes, so, the shot was from that building, as the other building has permanently fixed glass windows and breaking any window would also mean an eye suspect though the shot was silenced.
Miles: Okay. So the shot was from that building, but how does that explain the type of the rifle?
Alex: Im starting to think you are a novice, detective Miles. Images of the wound show the exact size of the bullet. And the distance of his body from the blood confirms that it was rather an M24, not an AWM.
Miles: All agreed. Except that Im a novice.
Alex: Oh please, 15 cases, 10 successful. That might be satisfactory, but the inadequate amount of numbers shows your lack of experience.
Miles: Hum, so you researched me.
Alex: Of course I did; Im not Sherlock Holmes.

They both reached the morgue with officer McGill to watch Mr. Smiths body more closely for any more details. Detective Miles has done a few courses on medical inspection, so he starts watching for bruises while Alex and officer McGill speak about the case.

McGill: Detective Alex, the OESZA writing on the road, the whole thing was planned by someone who knew exactly where he would be that day.
Alex: Yes. And for that, Id need the CCTV footage.
McGill: I already saw that but found nothing to get on to. The angle of the CCTV doesnt cover the exact place. There are about a thousand people buying things in that street.
Alex: Oh, no. I want the one on a block before, the connecting street with the main road. McGill: Okay. But, what will you do with that?
Alex: I have to see something. Miles, found anything?
Miles: No. The body is clean, no bruises, nothing.
Alex: Okay.

The CCTV footage arrived, and Alex saw it until they got Mr. Smith on the footage.

Alex: There. Thats all we need.
McGill: What?
Alex: That small speed breaker, its too hard to notice. I noticed that detective Miles here tripped on that. If he hadnt, I also wouldve. Its just too hard to notice.
Miles: Respectfully agreed.
McGill: Wait a minute, I also tripped there.
Alex: See the footage. About 2 in 5 people trip on that breaker. Point proved.
McGill: And those who dont?
Miles: Their locales, or maybe they walk on this route every day.
Alex: Yes, exactly. Mr. Smiths office isnt that way; his home is also not this way. But, he used this road every day; for some reason. And the killer knew that. Which means he followed him for a few days to know his routine.
McGill: But my question is, why exactly here?
Miles: Oh, detective Alex already figured that out. OESZA is trying to make a name for itself.
McGill: Oh, God, I hope not.
Alex: Come on, lets find the building where he was shot from.

They again arrive at the crime scene. Detective Alex stands at the point of the shot to find out the exact angle.

Miles: Thats impossible, mate.
Alex: Ah, Im not your mate.
Miles: Oh yes, you are, detective Ivaraf.
Alex: Youre not supposed to know that name.
Miles: Well, now I do.
Alex: Okay, no big deal, you googled me. Congratulations! Now you know Im a great detective.
Miles: Yes. But would you even tell that yourself after the Berlin triple murder case?
Alex: (Shocked) thats not in google, is it?
Miles: No. But Im not going to tell you how I got it.
Alex: Ill find out myself.
Miles: Yeah, good luck with that.

They went to the third floor and stopped.

Alex: The shot mustve been from 3D-
Miles: 3F, or 4D or 4F.
Alex: Yes.
Miles: Now?
Alex: Now we observe the people in these rooms. Because I believe anyone from these four flats is directly related to the murder.

They knock at each of the flats and found two families at 3D and 3F. Families cant be related to this, so they move on to 4D and 4F. 4D was locked with no one living, so it was more or less suspicious, but they decided to check on that after confirming whether it was 4F or not. A person opened the door after they knocked.

4F guy: Yes, how may I help you?
Alex: Were from your ISP, and we need to check your rooms wires as we think there might be a leak, and we need to find that.
4F guy: But I use cellular data.
Alex: Oh. In that case, there still might be a problem with the wires. Can we just check?

They checked the window, too, while acting like checking the wires. They got out without letting him know that they werent from the ISP.

Miles: (On the corridor) what do you think?
Alex: He might be our guy.
Miles: But I saw no traces of gunpowder.
Alex: Yes, but I know that. But else you didnt saw?
Miles: What?
Alex: Dust. This weeks weather has been quite windy in Dublin, and the wind flows with speed through exactly this Northeast angle in this area, which is supposed to bring dust from outside.
Miles: Wow, um, maybe he cleaned it.
Alex: Oh, come on, hes 25 max and lives alone. People dont clean dust unless they have OCD, which he doesnt, telling by how his room was.
Miles: So youre confident.
Alex: Yeah, but just got to make sure of one more thing first. Since when he has been staying and where he works. Just to confirm.

They asked the building owner for more details on the 4F guy, and they got to know that he got in the apartment just a week ago and told them that hed leave after a couple of months. They came out and started thinking.

Miles: Renting this flat for two months is too long for this case, Alex.
Alex: No, he has to at least avoid suspicion from the cops. Hes being paid in full for the stay by OESZA.
Miles: So hes not the shooter?
Alex: No. His body isnt the type who could shoot an M24 on a professional level. But he did look worried when we were checking the windows, you saw?
Miles: Yeah. We should arrest this guy.

They went up and knocked on 4F, but no one opened. After waiting for 3 minutes, Miles broke the lock. As supposed, no one was inside. They quickly looked down the window and could find the 4F guy sliding through the pipe and started running away. So they both went after him climbing down the stairs. The chase was on. But he was getting far away. So detective Miles stopped and aimed his gun at him and shot. But he missed, and the guy ran away into more people in a busy street. They searched, but couldnt find him, so they went back to Dublin PD.

McGill: Andy OBrian. Not from this city. But we will find him.
Miles: He heard us and ran away.
McGill: Dont worry, well get him.
Alex: But you wont get any information off him.
McGill: Why?
Alex: Because youll find him dead. If they could kill the MP in public, theyd kill him anywhere, just not to risk the leakage of any more information about their gang.
McGill: Oh. But I disagree with you, Mr. Alex. And supposing even if they intend to do that, I believe well find him before they can.
Alex: Lets just wait and watch.

MAKE A DEDUCTION

OFFICER McGILL IS RIGHT

Wrong.

DETECTIVE ALEX IS RIGHT

Detective Alex was right.

The boy was found dead the same day. Though they couldnt get anything on OESZA, officer McGill saved his job after declaring the boy as one of the killers in the press briefing. Alex and Miles stayed in Dublin and further investigated the boys body, but this time he was stabbed. OESZA remains a complete mystery.
